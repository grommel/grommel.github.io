Research Overview

My research focuses on gravitational effects in brane-world models, including domain wall structures, localization of fermions and gauge fields, and extra-dimensional geometry. I explore how fields interact with warped spacetimes in higher dimensions and contribute to the theoretical foundation of modern cosmological and field-theoretic models.

Selected Publications

Fermions Localization on de Sitter Branes

Rommel Guerrero & R. Omar Rodriguez (2019)

This work explores how spin-1/2 particles (fermions) can be confined on a dynamic four-dimensional de Sitter (dS4) brane embedded in a five-dimensional universe with different curvatures on either side. The brane is generated by a domain wall that connects a de Sitter space (dS5) and an anti-de Sitter space (AdS5). A specially designed nonminimal Yukawa interaction between the fermions and the scalar field of the wall allows for localization of a single massless chiral fermion mode—regardless of how thick or thin the wall is.

The paper shows that massive fermion states appear in two categories: a discrete set of bound states below a certain energy threshold (the mass gap), and a continuum of resonant states above it. As the asymmetry of the wall increases, both the number and mass of bound fermion states also increase. Remarkably, this localization mechanism works even in the limit of a "thin brane", where the wall becomes infinitely narrow.

The study uses methods from supersymmetric quantum mechanics to analyze the fermion spectra and shows that the localization of both gravity and fermions share similar features. References include key works by Randall and Sundrum on brane gravity, as well as follow-up studies exploring thick branes, dynamic metrics, and fermion trapping mechanisms.
