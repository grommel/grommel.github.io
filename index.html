I am a physicist with a Ph.D. in Fundamental Physics and over 18 years of university-level teaching and research experience across Venezuela, Ecuador, and the United States. My academic work has focused on the gravitational effects of brane-world scenarios, including domain walls, warped geometries, and the localization of gauge and fermionic fields. This research contributes to the broader understanding of extra-dimensional gravity and field dynamics.

As a professor, I have taught a wide range of physics courses—Classical Mechanics, Electromagnetism, Quantum Mechanics, Statistical Physics, and more—at both undergraduate and graduate levels. My teaching emphasizes conceptual clarity, theoretical rigor, and the integration of computational tools such as Mathematica, Maple, Python, and Excel. I have supervised numerous undergraduate and master’s theses and mentored students who later pursued Ph.D. studies abroad.

I am deeply committed to student-centered education, the advancement of scientific knowledge, and fostering intellectual curiosity through teaching, research, and academic collaboration.
