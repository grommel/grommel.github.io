I am a physicist with a Ph.D. in Fundamental Physics and over 18 years of university-level teaching and research experience across Venezuela, Ecuador, and the United States. My academic work has focused on gravitational effects in brane-world scenarios, including domain walls, warped geometries, and the localization of gauge and fermionic fields. I have published extensively in peer-reviewed journals such as Physical Review D and the Revista Mexicana de Física, contributing to the theoretical understanding of extra-dimensional gravity and field dynamics.

As a professor, I have taught a wide range of physics courses—Classical Mechanics, Electromagnetism, Quantum Mechanics, Statistical Physics, and more—at both undergraduate and graduate levels. My teaching is grounded in clarity, conceptual depth, and integration of computational tools like Mathematica, Maple, Python, and Excel. I have guided numerous undergraduate and master's thesis projects, mentored students who went on to pursue Ph.D. studies in Europe, and received multiple teaching excellence awards.

I am deeply committed to student-centered education, scientific rigor, and the dissemination of physics knowledge through teaching, research, and academic outreach.
